Who am I?

My name is Andrea Kouta Dagnino. I'm a final year mathematics student at the Open University, taking M337 (Complex analysis), MS327 (Deterministic & Stochastic dynamics), MST326 (Fluid dynmics) and SXP390 (Research project). After my degree I intend to pursue a masters degree in mathematical/theoretical physics, perhaps specializing in condensed matter physics. Outside of my university studies I like to record lecture videos on my youtube channel Fisika lectures.

Research

Thermalisation in Quantum systems

I am greatly interested in scientific research and would like to pursue a career in academia in the future. My research interests mostly lie in theoretical physics, more specifically condensed matter physics. Indeed since May 2021 I have been undertaking a research project under the supervision of Dr Andrew J.A. James and Dr Jim P. Hague on the thermalization of Extended Bose-Hubbard models. A preliminary research report presenting the main results from this project can be found here.

Topological matter

I am also interested in the applications of topology in condensed matter physics. This interest is not jus theoretical, I have been working during the past weeks on an expository essay surveying the connection between the Berry phase, the Quantum Hall effect and topological insulators. I have also written some code in python which is capable of calculating the Chern number for a band in a given Hamiltonian. The essay (together with some other ones I'm working on) can be found here.

Essays

I have also compiled a few expository essays on topics in theoretical physics that have spiked my interest. These include
1) Geometric phase, the Quantum Hall effect and Topological insulators: in this essay I investigate the importance of geometric phase in the Quantum Hall effect and in Topological insulators. I provide a derivation of the Berry phase and the Adiabatic theorem and go on to discuss how it can be applied in the context of Quantum field theory to model the behaviour of electrons subject to strong magnetic fields, and to materials manifesting topologically protected phases of matter.
3) The Unruh effect: in this essay I derive the Unruh effect. I am working on adding another section discussing the implications of the Unruh radiation on the observer-dependence of quantum entanglement.
2) Geometric/Deformation quantisation (in progress): in this essay I investigate the beautiful correspondence between the laws of classical physics and quantum physics from a geometrical perspective. I look at how one can quantize the symplectic structure on which Hamiltonian mechanics is performed to get a I then explain how this quantization procedure leads to the laws of quantum mechanics we know today. This deformation leads to new insights into the differential geometry of the Berry phase and other fields of physics.
